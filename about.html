PHILOSOPHY

Brutalism in web design is a reaction against the artificial smoothness of the modern web.

We live in an era of templates. Soft shadows, rounded corners, and polite micro-interactions have homogenized the internet. Brutalism rejects this. It embraces the raw materials of the web: clear structure, default fonts, and high-contrast colors.

"IF IT FEELS COMFORTABLE, IT'S NOT BRUTALISM."

ORIGINS

Derived from the architectural movement of the 1950s (Beton Brut), digital brutalism focuses on honesty of materials. It treats the screen as a flat surface, not a window into three-dimensional reality (despite our use of fake 3D for impact).
